Default Case Study Guide: Apex Electro-Tech

Context: Apex Electro-Tech produces smart IoT hubs. Due to rising customer complaints and internal rework loops, profitability has declined. Follow the guided sequence below to see how each tool is used:

Step 0 (Symptom & COPQ): Apex selects *High defects*. They analyze their COPQ (Revenue: ₹100 Cr). Visible External Failures are ₹4.2 Cr, while hidden Internal Failures reach ₹6.8 Cr. Appraisal costs are ₹2.5 Cr and Prevention is ₹0.8 Cr.
Step 1 (Check Sheet): The team logs counts for 5 main defects over 4 process stations. "Solder Joint Fracture" is logged with 65 total occurrences, the highest on the list.
Step 2 (Pareto): The compiled Check Sheet is sorted. Solder Fractures represent 40.9% of defects. The "Vital Few" cutoff points to Solder Joint Fracture, Calibration Drift, and Enclosure Scratch (accounting for 81.1% of total errors).
Step 3 (Fishbone & 5-Why): Focus is set to *Solder Joint Fracture*. A 5-Why trace reveals reflow oven zones are shifting due to an unstandardized thermocouple calibration schedule.
Step 4 (Histogram): Peak reflow oven temperature is measured. LSL is 235°C, USL is 245°C. With a Mean of 238.4°C and Std Dev of 1.48°C, the process is found **Not Capable** ($C_{pk} = 0.77$).
Step 5 (Scatter): Conveyor speed is plotted against Peak temperature, revealing a strong negative correlation ($r = -0.98$) between conveyor speed and thermal joints.
Step 6 (Control Chart): Tracking hourly oven peak temperatures reveals that the process is out of control at hour 9 and hour 19 due to external electrical loading fluctuations.
Step 7 (Flowchart): The process map identifies hand-soldering assembly (Station 2) as generating 75.6% of solder fractures.
